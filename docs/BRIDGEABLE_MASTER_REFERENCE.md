# Part 1 — Vision & Strategy

## 1.1 Mission Statement

Bridgeable exists to make exploitation structurally impossible for the physical economy. Every product, every feature, every business decision is evaluated against this mission. If it doesn't serve the people who build, maintain, and bury America — it doesn't get built.

## 1.2 The Core Insight

Small and medium businesses in the physical economy are systematically underserved by enterprise software built for companies ten times their size, overcharged for generic tools that ignore their industry context, and excluded from financial services that assume they're too small or too risky to serve profitably.

Bridgeable replaces fragmented, generic vendor relationships with an integrated, industry-specific platform where every feature shares the same data layer, the same intelligence, and the same financial infrastructure.

**The broader mission the physical economy revealed:**

Bridgeable was conceived before the physical economy focus crystallized. The original insight was simpler and larger:

*Small and medium businesses are systematically overcharged for enterprise software built for companies ten times their size, or forced to cobble together disconnected tools that create their own operational burden. Neither option is acceptable. Both are exploitative in their own way.*

The physical economy became Phase 1 because the design partners were personal, the cross-tenant network effects were strongest, and the financial services opportunity was clearest there. But the problem is universal.

Every SMB owner faces the same choice: (A) overpay for enterprise software designed for someone 10x their size, (B) piece together 8-12 tools that don't talk to each other and create their own back-office burden, or (C) use spreadsheets and hope.

Bridgeable's vault architecture, intelligence layer, and Business Services stack are industry-agnostic by design. The verticals are the sequence in which the problem gets solved — starting where the personal relationships, network effects, and financial services opportunity are strongest, expanding outward as each vertical proves the model.

At full scale, Bridgeable is not merely the connective tissue of the physical economy supply chain — it is the SMB operating system that makes enterprise-grade infrastructure accessible to every business regardless of size, industry, or geography, and the infrastructure through which the political and economic interests of the people who build, serve, and sustain America are organized, represented, and protected.

### The GEICO Model Applied to the Physical Economy

Berkshire Hathaway didn't buy GEICO because it was a good insurance company. They bought it because GEICO had a structural data advantage that compounded over time into a cost structure no competitor could replicate.

GEICO's direct-to-consumer model meant they owned the customer relationship and the claims data. Their underwriting got more accurate every year. The longer they operated the cheaper they could price while maintaining margins. Competitors couldn't follow because they didn't have the data to price accurately enough to match GEICO's rates profitably.

The moat wasn't the brand. The moat wasn't the advertising gecko. The moat was the data that made every subsequent decision more accurate than any competitor.

**Bridgeable is the GEICO model applied to the physical economy.** The arrangement conference — the moment inside the funeral home where every death care product sale is actually made — is Bridgeable's equivalent of GEICO's claims data. No platform has ever been inside that room. Every case makes the intelligence more accurate. It compounds permanently.

What makes the arrangement conference data unique: every ordering portal sees what got ordered; every ERP sees what got produced and shipped; every analytics platform sees who visited the website. Nobody has ever seen what happened inside the arrangement conference — what the family was shown, what they responded to, what made them say yes, what made them hesitate, what the funeral director did differently when they converted vs. when they didn't.

The compounding advantage means that by Year 5, with tens of thousands of cases, the intelligence layer predicts what families will choose before they choose it. A competitor starting fresh would need years of cases to reach equivalent intelligence. The gap compounds permanently.

The switching cost isn't a fee or a contract. It's making your business meaningfully worse in ways you feel every single day.

Bridgeable's walls are largely invisible because the cross-tenant network IS the value. A funeral home connected to their vault manufacturer, their cemetery, and their crematory through one platform isn't caged — they're more connected to their partners than they've ever been.

## 3.10 Bridgeable Intelligence — The Unified AI Layer

All AI capabilities in Bridgeable live in one layer. There is no isolated per-feature AI. The Arrangement Scribe, Call Overlay, Compliance Monitor, Conversational Production Planner, Legacy Compositor, Quoting Engine extraction, Bid Monitoring Agent, Drawing Parser, Wall Design Engineer — these are all tasks handed to the same intelligence layer, which queries and writes to the same vault (Core).

**The correct mental model:**

```
NOT this:
  Quoting AI        (knows quotes only)
  Scribe AI         (knows cases only)
  Compliance AI     (knows compliance only)
  Call AI           (knows calls only)

THIS:
  Bridgeable Intelligence
  (knows everything in the Core vault)
  ↓ receives tasks:
  Quoting task          → draws on orders, inventory, production schedule
  Scribe task           → draws on case history, prior arrangements
  Compliance task       → draws on all documents, expiry events, audit history
  Call overlay task     → draws on full account vault in real time
  Bid monitoring task   → draws on catalog, pricing, production capacity
  Drawing extraction    → draws on SKU library, historical quote patterns
  Onboarding task       → draws on imported data to seed all other tasks
```

**Why this matters architecturally:**

Because the intelligence layer sees everything in the vault, cross-domain awareness is automatic:

- Quoting agent knows current production capacity when generating a quote lead time
- Bid monitoring agent checks inventory before flagging an opportunity as viable
- Call overlay knows whether a referenced job has an open quote, an outstanding balance, a delivery issue — without being told to look
- Onboarding data import immediately seeds quoting extraction vocabulary, bid monitoring filters, and compliance calendars — not just populates tables

**Path to Bridgeable Intelligence as a product:**

When Bridgeable Intelligence is productized as a named native layer (per the roadmap in section 7.3), it is not a rebuild. It is a rebrand and refinement of something already battle-tested across every vertical, with self-learning data accumulated from day one. Every AI feature built against this pattern contributes to that eventual product.

**Development rule:**
No new AI feature is built in isolation. Every AI capability is a task definition that specifies: what context to pull from the vault, what prompt/extraction logic to apply, and where to write the output back. The intelligence layer executes it. This applies to all verticals, all tenants, all future features.

## 3.11 QuotingEngine — Universal Quoting Service

The QuotingEngine is a core platform service, not a vertical-specific feature. Every vertical and every product line consumes it. The AI extraction, bid monitoring, drawing parsing, and pricing logic are built once and configured per product line.

### Four Components

**Component 1 — Intake Layer**

Multiple input methods, all producing the same internal quote payload in the vault:

```
Manual entry          → line items entered directly by rep
Drawing upload        → PDF/CAD → AI extracts items + quantities  
Bid board scrape      → listing URL/text → AI extracts relevant items
Sketcher output       → Redi-Rock wall design → auto-generates block takeoff
Catalog selection     → browse products, add to quote
Voice/conversational  → natural language → structured line items
```

All six methods output a structured VaultItem of type `quote`.

**Component 2 — Extraction Layer (AI core)**

Powered by Bridgeable Intelligence. Product line schema defines the extraction vocabulary. The extraction agent is the same across all verticals — only the vocabulary changes.

Pipeline:
```
Input arrives →
Extraction agent runs against product line schema →
Line items identified with confidence scores →
Low confidence items flagged for human review →
High confidence items auto-populated →
Quote draft created as VaultItem
```

Confidence scoring is a first-class UI feature:
```
✅ 12" x 8' Corrugated HDPE Pipe   qty: 24   [98% — auto-populated]
✅ 12" Band Coupler                 qty: 23   [94% — auto-populated]
⚠️  End Section (assumed 12")       qty: 2    [71% — verify before sending]
❓ "Special fitting per detail 4"   qty: 1    [needs manual review]
```

Quotes are never sent with unreviewed low-confidence items. The review UX is fast enough that even 85% accuracy saves significant rep time vs. manual entry.

Drawing parser specifically:
```
Upload PDF drawing →
Vision model reads drawing →
Identifies every item matching product line catalog →
Quantities pulled from drawing notes/schedules first, then plan view count →
Cross-referenced against SKU library →
Draft quote line items generated →
Unmatched items flagged: "found '15" RCP' — no matching SKU, needs review"
```

**Component 3 — Bid Monitoring Agent**

Scheduled background agent (powered by Bridgeable Intelligence). Runs against configured external sources (Syracuse Builders Exchange, state DOT listings, county procurement boards, private bid exchanges, etc.).

```
Agent runs on schedule →
Scrapes configured sources →
Filters by geography, project type, product categories →
For each relevant listing:
  → Extracts scope items matching tenant catalog
  → Generates draft quote as VaultItem
  → Scores opportunity (size, proximity, estimated value)
  → Creates vault notification: "New bid opportunity — details + draft quote ready"
  → Notifies sales team
Sales rep reviews draft quote →
One-click send to contractor portal
```

The rep never touches a spreadsheet. They review and approve.

**Component 4 — Pricing and Output Layer**

```
Pricing rules per product line (cost + margin, tiered volume, contractor discounts)
Accessory/requirement auto-suggestions per product line specs
Availability check against current inventory vault
Lead time from production schedule vault
Quote document generated via Bridgeable Documents (see section 3.12)
Contractor portal link generated — they can view, approve, request changes
On approval → auto-converts to sales order VaultItem → existing pipeline
```

### Self-Learning Loop

The QuotingEngine improves continuously via a structured feedback loop:

Every correction a rep makes to a draft quote is logged:
```json
{
  "input_type": "drawing",
  "original_extraction": "12in HDPE pipe, qty 20",
  "corrected_to": "12in HDPE pipe, qty 24",
  "correction_type": "quantity_error",
  "product_line": "wastewater",
  "confidence_at_extraction": 0.71
}
```

Two learning mechanisms:
- **Short-term (few-shot):** Recent corrections fed back into extraction prompts immediately. No retraining required.
- **Long-term (fine-tuning):** Accumulated correction data used to periodically fine-tune extraction model on tenant's actual drawings and catalog.

Win/loss signal: Whether a sent quote results in a won job is the strongest learning signal. The bid monitoring agent learns which sources, geographies, and project types yield real opportunities vs. noise.

Expected accuracy trajectory:
```
Month 1:   ~70% — significant rep review needed
Month 3:   ~85% — minor corrections
Month 6:   ~93% — most quotes send with one glance
Month 12:  97%+ — only genuinely unusual specs need review
```

### The Competitive Moat

A competitor starting fresh takes years to reach the accuracy of a system trained on a tenant's specific catalog, drawing conventions, and regional bid patterns. The self-learning loop means the longer a tenant uses the platform, the more expensive it is to leave.

# Part 8 — Future Roadmap

## 8.1 Bridgeable Communications — Native Communications Platform

**Current state:** RingCentral (VoIP) + Deepgram (transcription) + Twilio (SMS) + Resend (email)
**Native target:** Bridgeable Communications — owned SIP infrastructure, SMS, email

**The intermediate step (implement now, delivers 90% of the value):**
```
RingCentral calls → transcribed by Deepgram → 
  transcript written to vault as communication VaultItem
Emails (forwarded/BCC'd to Bridgeable) → parsed → VaultItem
SMS via Twilio → VaultItem
```

Every communication becomes a first-class VaultItem immediately, without owning the infrastructure.

**What a communication VaultItem looks like:**
```
item_type: "communication"
channel: "call" | "sms" | "email"
direction: "inbound" | "outbound"
participants: [linked CRM VaultItems]
transcript: full text
sentiment: scored
topics_mentioned: [extracted by intelligence layer]
vault_items_referenced: [auto-linked orders, cases, quotes, invoices mentioned]
action_items: [extracted]
outcome: logged
cross_tenant_visible: true/false
```

**What native communications unlocks:**

1. **Complete relationship context** — When anyone contacts a tenant, the intelligence layer surfaces their complete vault before the conversation begins. Every prior call, order, case, quote, complaint, payment. No CRM data entry required.

2. **Supercharged call overlay** — The call overlay already surfaces vault items as topics are mentioned. With every communication as a VaultItem, it also surfaces prior conversation threads. Rep picks up a call knowing the last three times this person called and why.

3. **Autonomous follow-up** — Intelligence layer monitors communication gaps:
   - Quote sent, no response in 5 days, no follow-up → drafts follow-up → rep approves
   - Contractor called, left voicemail, no callback in 4 hours → notifies rep with full context pre-loaded
   - Funeral home asked about pricing on a call, no quote generated in 48 hours → flags to sales team

4. **Cross-tenant communication history** — When a funeral home calls a manufacturer, both sides see the same communication VaultItem. The manufacturer rep knows the last three orders, open delivery issues, and payment status before the director explains anything.

5. **Communication intelligence at scale** — Across all tenants, the intelligence layer identifies: which call patterns convert to orders, which follow-up timing works, which objections precede quote rejection, which communication patterns correlate with churn. Improves platform-wide over time.

6. **Compliance and audit** — Every communication automatically archived. FTC audit, dispute resolution, employee review, and training all have complete records without manual logging.

7. **The funeral home relationship implication** — A family calls three years after a service. The director pulls the family vault. Every communication since the arrangement is there. The director knows their name, their loved one, what they asked last time. That experience is not possible with any other platform.

**The native infrastructure path:**
Owning SIP + email + SMS infrastructure is a later-stage investment. The intermediate step of writing all communications to the vault delivers the relationship intelligence immediately. When native infrastructure is built, it is a backend swap — the VaultItem model and intelligence layer don't change.
